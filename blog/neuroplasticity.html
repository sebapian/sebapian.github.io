Neuroplasticity

17/07/2022

How Understanding the Principles of Neuroplasticity Benefits People

Neuroplasticity describes a brain’s ability to grow and adapt through experiences. Breaking the word down, neuro refers to neurons or nerve cells that are fundamental units of the brain and nervous system. It transforms and relay electrical signals when receiving sensory input from the external world and sending motor commands to our muscles. Plasticity on the other hand refers to something of elasticity and flexibility, defined as being easily influenced or trained. Combining these two terms, neurons are adaptable.

Neurons that utilised frequently develop stronger connections whereas those that are not rarely or never used eventually die. As there are limited capacities in our brains, the needed adaptability of the brain in a changing environment will prune away the weak neurons to make way for new connections.

How does neuroplasticity benefit people? Adaptability is key for the brain and the changing structure of neurons enables:

  • The ability to learn new things
  • Recovery from brain events like strokes or traumatic injuries
  • Rewire functions in the brain and body(loss of sight as an example, sense of hearing may heighten)
  • Wide range of enhanced cognitive abilities

Methods to Increase Neuroplasticity

  1. Enriching your Environment

    2. Learning environments have shown to simulate positive changes to the brain. This may range from learning a new language, travelling, learning to play an instrument, and reading. Activities such as these enhances connectivity between brain regions and help form new neural networks.

  2. Meditation

    3. Recent studies have shown that several areas of the brain become larger after meditation, such as the hippocampus and the fight or flight system in the amygdala.

  3. Get proper amount of Sleep

    4. Sleep promotes the growth of dendrites in brain neurons, which are the end of neurons that help transmit information from one neuron to the next. Strengthening these connections may increase our brain plasticity.

  4. Regular Exercise

    5. There are plenty of benefits to how exercising can benefit our neuroplasticity: Prevent neuron loss in key areas of the hippocampus, a part of the brain involved in memory; Improves functional connectivity in the basal ganglia, the part of the brain responsible for motor control and learning.

Growth Mindset

Understanding neuroplasticity is crucial because it enables one to believe that our brain capabilities are not static, but only ever-growing. This opens one’s doorway to many possibilities, believing they can learn anything and that their intelligence can grow through effort and actons. That is what it means to have a Growth Mindset! The counter to this mindset is Fixed Mindset – believing that intelligence is fixed and something innate.

A growth mindset recognises that setbacks are a necessary part of the learning process and are motivated by challenges. View of failures are temporary and changeable, crucial for learning, resilience and performance.
